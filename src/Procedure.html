The Dual Motor Electric Vehicle Test Rig

A novel dual motor electric vehicle test rig with mechanical loading designed to carry out the performance analysis of the dual motor propulsion system was designed. The figure shows the overall arrangement of the test rig. It includes two BLDC motors connected in parallel to a common battery pack. The two motors are connected to a common mechanical loading system. The two motors and the common loading shaft were supported on a custom designed mild steel stand. Each motor is connected independently to a throttle. Using this arrangement, the power delivered to the two motors can be manipulated based on our convenience. They also have their own independent controllers. Such a system would allow us to analyze the working a dual motor electric vehicle more easily and also help us understand the key areas for improvement.

Varible Load Test

  1. The surrounding ambient environment was analysed and the ambient temperature was noted. Initially, the battery voltage was determined using a multimeter and the reading was noted.
  2. Initially the no load values of current, voltage and power were noted from the energy meters of both motors.
  3. 1 kg was added to the dynamometer and the throttle of Motor 1 was rotated until the pre-determined rated speed of the motor was attained. Once the rated speed was attained, the corresponding voltage, current and power readings were noted from the two energy meters.
  4. Loads were added to the dynamometer in a increment of 1 kg and the throttle was adjusted to maintain the rated speed. The corresponding voltage and current readings were gathered from the energy meter.
  5. The corresponding spring balance reading was noted for various speeds.
  6. This process would continue until the first motor began to stall.
  7. Now, the first motor would be held at full throttle while the throttle of the second motor would be adjusted to maintain the rated speed.
  8. This process would continue until both the motors stall.

Varible Speed Test

  1. The surrounding ambient environment was analysed and the ambient temperature was noted. Initially, the battery voltage was determined using a multimeter and the reading was noted.
  2. The load at which maximum efficiency was obtained at the rated speed was identified. This load was applied on the test rig.
  3. Keeping this load constant, The speed was varied with an increment of 50 rpm.
  4. The corresponding voltage and current readings were gathered from the energy meter for various speeds.
  5. The corresponding spring balance reading was noted for various speeds.
  6. This process would continue until the motor is unable to increase its speed furthur. In this case the second motor would be used to increase the speed.
  7. This process would continue until the maximum possible speed was attained.

Coasting Time

  1. The surrounding ambient environment was analysed and the ambient temperature was noted. Initially, the battery voltage was determined using a multimeter and the reading was noted.
  2. Initially the no load values of current, voltage and power were noted from the energy meters of both motors.
  3. 1 kg was added to the dynamometer and the throttle of Motor 1 was rotated until the pre-determined rated speed of the motor was attained.
  4. Once the rated speed was attained, the throttle was released and the time taken for the motor to reach 0 rpm from the rated rpm was noted.
  5. Loads were added to the dynamometer in a increment of 1 kg and the throttle was adjusted to maintain the rated speed. the corresponding voltage and current readings were gathered from the energy meters.
  6. This process would continue until the first motor began to stall.
  7. Now, the first motor would be held at full throttle while the throttle of the second motor would be adjusted to maintain the rated speed.
  8. This process would continue until both the motors stall.
